CiGNN: A Community-induced Graph Neural Network

Authors: Shi Yin Hong and Lu Zhang

Limitations in the expressive power of message-passing neural networks (MPNNs) are mitigated by the recent proposal for subgraph Graph Neural Network (GNN) models, which extend local aggregation of MPNNs by upgrading node representations to respective subgraph representations. However, subgraph GNN models have limited interpretability in capturing inherent local structural dynamics across diverse graph structures. In this work, we present a framework for subgraph GNNs, termed Community-Induced Graph Neural Network (CiGNN), that endows an intuitive interpretability basis for subgraph GNNs by capturing the dynamics of inherent structural community topology in subgraph representation realization relative to individual nodes. We propose four variants of CiGNN in establishing graph representations from community-induced local subgraphs. Specifically, we approach subgraph formulation from a statistical physics perspective combined with network analysis leveraging a theoretical basis from the Reichardt and Bornholdt's Potts (RBP) model and Louvain-based community optimization methods. Our modular framework supports multiple mainstream GNN base models, making CiGNN a general module easily adaptable to existing GNN or subgraph GNN models with intuitive interpretability. Experimental results on six benchmark graph classification datasets show that CiGNN achieves competitive performance against subgraph GNN baselines and recent state-of-the-art GNNs.
